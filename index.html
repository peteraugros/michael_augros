About Michael

Michael Augros makes the deepest questions clear, for a world that has begun asking them again. God, the soul, what it means to be human in the age of the machine. He has spent his life learning to answer them with rigor, warmth, and uncommon clarity.

A professor at Thomas Aquinas College (where he teaches across the curriculum, today on the New England campus after many years in California), Michael has spent his career drawing on Aristotle and St. Thomas Aquinas to illuminate the questions that matter most: Does God exist? What is the human soul? Can a machine ever truly think? He is the author of Who Designed the Designer? and The Immortal in You, and has spoken everywhere from the Oxford Union to parishes, conferences, and classrooms across the country.

What sets Michael apart is not only the rigor of his thought but his rare gift for delivery: the ability to take a dense argument and hand it to an audience whole, with clarity, warmth, and humor. Students and organizers say the same thing again and again: they came expecting a lecture and left having seen something come alive.
